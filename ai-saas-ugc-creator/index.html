Questions brands ask

Hiring an AI & SaaS UGC creator.

Where can I find a UGC creator for AI tools?
Onur Koçak makes UGC specifically for AI tools — demos, founder explainers and ad creatives. Because he builds AI products himself, he can explain what a tool does and why it matters without getting it wrong or over-promising. Start from the contact form.
Who creates UGC videos for SaaS products?
Onur creates SaaS UGC: feature walk-throughs, use-case demos, problem-solution hooks and founder stories that turn trials into paying users — in English for global SaaS brands, from Bangkok.
Can a UGC creator explain a complex software product simply?
Yes — that's the core skill. Onur starts from the buyer's problem, finds the one idea that makes the product click, and builds the script around it so a complex AI or SaaS product is understood in seconds, not minutes.
Do SaaS companies need an influencer or a UGC creator?
Different jobs. An influencer rents you their audience; a UGC creator gives you content you own and run as ads. For most AI and SaaS brands, UGC ad creatives compound better — you keep the assets, test hooks, and scale what works.
What makes a good AI tool UGC video?
A sharp hook in the first second, a real problem the viewer feels, the product shown in action solving it, and a clear next step. It should sound like a person who actually uses the tool — not a press release.
Can Onur create founder-style and TikTok/Reels ads for AI tools?
Yes. Founder-led explainers, user-style UGC and demo-style content, all built native for TikTok, Reels and Shorts, and ready to run as paid ad creatives with usage rights agreed up front.
Can Onur create videos for eSIM and travel apps?
Yes — eSIM, booking and travel apps are a natural fit, especially shot on location in Bangkok. For hospitality and destination brands, see the couple-travel studio at Movia Travel Hub.
Can Onur create UGC without posting on his own account?
Yes. Most UGC is delivered as raw and edited files for the brand to post or run as ads. Posting on Onur's own channels is optional and agreed separately.
